## How to Generate an Email

### Step 1: Select Email Template

Choose from 6 professional templates:

1. **Follow-up Email** - After meetings or inspections
2. **Estimate Email** - Send quotes and proposals
3. **Insurance Communication** - Contact adjusters or companies
4. **Thank You Email** - Post-sale appreciation
5. **Schedule Meeting** - Arrange appointments
6. **Custom Email** - Any other purpose

### Step 2: Add Context (Optional but Recommended)

**The more context you provide, the better the email!**

**Good context includes:**
- Customer name
- Property address
- What was discussed
- Specific details to mention
- Next steps or action items
- Timeline or deadlines
- Claim numbers (for insurance)
- Any special considerations

### Step 3: Generate

1. Click **"Generate Email"** button
2. Wait 3-5 seconds for AI generation
3. Review the email

### Step 4: Edit & Send

1. **Read the generated email**
2. **Edit if needed** - personalize, adjust tone, add details
3. **Copy to clipboard** - click the copy button
4. **Paste into your email client** (Gmail, Outlook, etc.)
5. **Send!**

### 2. Estimate Email
**Use when:** Sending quotes or proposals

**Include:**
- Customer name
- Property details
- Estimate amount/range
- What's included

- Expiration date

**Example context:**
```

**Use when:** Contacting adjusters or insurance companies

```

adjuster to review hail damage estimate. Have photos and
measurements ready. Requesting site visit next week."

## Pro Tips for Better Emails

### 1. Add Specific Details
‚ùå **Vague:** "Customer wants roof done"
‚úÖ **Specific:** "Sarah Johnson at 456 Oak St wants architectural shingles in Weathered Wood color, 2,200 sq ft, start date after June 15th"

### 2. Mention Timeline
Always include when things will happen:
- "I'll send the estimate by Friday"
- "We can start work next Tuesday"
- "Installation takes 3-5 days"
- "I'll follow up on Monday"

### 3. Include Numbers
Reference specific details:
- Claim numbers
- Square footage
- Pricing
- Timeline
- Phone numbers
- Addresses

### 4. Set Clear Next Steps
End every email with what happens next:
- "I'll call you Friday to discuss the estimate"
- "Please review and let me know if you have questions"
- "Once you approve, we'll schedule installation"

### 5. Keep Context Conversational
Don't worry about perfect grammar in your context - the AI will clean it up!

‚ùå **Too formal context:** "The customer, Ms. Johnson, has indicated a preference for..."
‚úÖ **Natural context:** "Sarah wants dark gray shingles, worried about cost, needs it done before winter"
