## Example Response

### Customer Says: "It's too expensive"

**üìù WHAT TO SAY:**
```
"I completely understand your concern, and many of our best
customers felt the same way initially. But here's what they
discovered - when you factor in our lifetime warranty, the
premium materials that last 50% longer, and the fact that we
handle all insurance paperwork saving you 20+ hours of hassle,
the real question becomes: can you afford NOT to invest in
quality? Let me show you exactly how this pays for itself in
the first 5 years alone..."
```

**üí° WHY IT WORKS:**
```
This validates their concern while reframing from cost to value.
It uses social proof ("best customers felt the same"), introduces
concrete benefits (warranty, materials, time savings), and shifts
the conversation to long-term ROI rather than upfront price.
The psychology here leverages loss aversion - they start thinking
about what they might lose by NOT investing.
```

**üéØ IF THEY STILL RESIST:**
```
Ask: "What's your biggest concern about the investment - the
upfront cost, or the monthly payment structure? We have financing
options that make this more affordable than you might think."
Then address their specific concern directly with numbers and
show them payment plans that fit their budget.
```

## How to Use Agnes Effectively

### Before Customer Conversations:
1. Open Agnes
2. Review common objection scripts
3. Practice responses out loud
4. Memorize key phrases
5. Adapt to your style

### During Active Calls:
1. Keep Agnes open on phone/tablet
2. When customer objects, quickly check script
3. Use Agnes's wording (adapted to your voice)
4. Follow the strategy provided

### After Difficult Calls:
1. Enter the objection you faced
2. See how Agnes would handle it
3. Compare to what you said
4. Learn for next time

## Pro Tips

### 1. Practice Makes Perfect
- Review scripts weekly
- Practice with colleagues
- Record yourself using scripts
- Refine to sound natural

### 2. Adapt to Your Style
Agnes gives you the framework - make it yours:
- Use your own words
- Match customer's tone
- Be authentic
- Keep the core message

### 3. Combine Responses
For multiple objections:
1. Use Agnes for each separately
2. Combine strategies
3. Address in order of importance

### 4. Learn the Psychology
Don't just memorize - **understand WHY it works:**
- Builds trust faster
- Helps you adapt on the fly
- Makes you confident
- Sounds more natural

## Common Objections & Quick Responses

### "Too Expensive"
**Key:** Value over cost, payment options, long-term savings

### "Need to Think About It"
**Key:** Create urgency, uncover real concern, next step commitment

### "Got Insurance Denial"
**Key:** Denials are common, appeal process, supplemental claims

### "Partial Payment"
**Key:** This is normal, work with customer, financing options

### "Want to Cancel"
**Key:** Address concerns, protect relationship, offer solutions

### "Got Other Quotes"
**Key:** Differentiate on value, don't compete on price alone
