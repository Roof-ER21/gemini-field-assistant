# How to Use Document Job Notes - Quick Field Note-Taking

## What is Document Job Notes?

A simple, fast note-taking tool for documenting job details, customer information, roof conditions, and next steps. Notes save directly to your Transcription page for easy access and analysis.

---

## When to Use

✅ **After inspections** - document findings
✅ **Customer meetings** - capture details
✅ **Job site observations** - quick notes
✅ **Customer contact info** - save for later
✅ **Roof conditions** - record damage
✅ **Next steps** - action items

---

## How to Use

### Step 1: Open Document Job
- Home page → Click **"Document Job"** (Quick Reference section)

### Step 2: Type Your Notes
Enter any job-related information:
- Customer name and contact
- Property address
- Roof condition observations
- Damage details
- Customer concerns
- Next steps
- Adjuster info
- Claim numbers
- Estimate amounts

### Step 3: Save to Transcription
1. Click **"Save to Transcription"**
2. See "Saved Successfully!" message
3. Note automatically clears for next entry

### Step 4: View in Transcription
1. Go to **Transcription** page
2. Your note appears at the top
3. Labeled as "Job Note - [timestamp]"
4. View alongside voice recordings

---

## Example Note

```
Customer contact: John Smith, 555-123-4567, john@email.com
Property: 123 Main St, Boston MA 02101

Roof condition: 20-year architectural shingles, installed 2008
Damage observed:
- Hail damage on north slope (15-20 impact points)
- Missing shingles near chimney (west side)
- Minor granule loss throughout
- No visible leaks reported by owner

Customer concerns:
- Cost of replacement
- Timeline (wants done before winter)
- Insurance coverage questions

Insurance: State Farm
Claim #: 12345
Adjuster: Sarah Jones, visiting Friday 2pm

Next steps:
- Send detailed estimate by Friday EOD
- Follow up Monday after adjuster visit
- Discuss financing options if needed
- Schedule installation for October

Estimate range: $8,500 - $12,000
Priority: High (pre-winter deadline)
```

## What to Include in Notes

### Essential Information:
1. **Customer Details**
   - Full name
   - Phone number
   - Email address
   - Property address

2. **Roof Observations**
   - Age of roof
   - Shingle type
   - Damage description
   - Problem areas
   - Overall condition

3. **Customer Concerns**
   - Main objections
   - Questions asked
   - Timeline needs
   - Budget concerns

4. **Insurance Info**
   - Company name
   - Claim number
   - Adjuster name/contact
   - Visit schedule

5. **Next Steps**
   - Your commitments
   - Follow-up dates
   - Documents to send
   - Calls to make

6. **Estimates**
   - Price range
   - What's included
   - Payment options
   - Timeline

## Pro Tips

### Quick Documentation
- Type while still on-site
- Fresh details = accurate notes
- Include specific numbers
- Note customer mood/attitude

### Use Template Format
Create your own template:
```
Customer:
Property:
Roof:
Damage:
Concerns:
Insurance:
Next Steps:
Estimate:
```
Copy/paste each time!

### Be Specific
❌ Vague: "Some damage"
✅ Specific: "15-20 hail impacts, north slope, 1-1.5 inch diameter"

### Include Timeline
Always note:
- When you'll follow up
- When estimate is due
- When work can start
- Customer's deadlines

## Workflows

### Workflow 1: Post-Inspection
1. Finish roof inspection
2. Sit in truck
3. Open Document Job
4. Type observations while fresh
5. Save to Transcription
6. Drive to next appointment

**Time: 3-5 minutes**

### Workflow 2: Customer Meeting
1. Meet with customer
2. Discuss project
3. After meeting, open Document Job
4. Document conversation highlights
5. Note next steps and commitments
6. Save for follow-up reference

**Time: 2-3 minutes**

### Workflow 3: Daily Recap
1. End of day
2. Open Document Job for each property visited
3. Quick notes on each
4. All saved to Transcription
5. Review tomorrow for follow-ups

**Time: 10-15 minutes total**

## Best Practices

### Do's ✅
- Document immediately after visit
- Be specific with details
- Include all contact info
- Note exact timelines
- Save estimate ranges
- Record customer mood/concerns

### Don'ts ❌
- Don't wait hours to document
- Don't be vague
- Don't skip contact details
- Don't forget next steps
- Don't lose track of commitments
