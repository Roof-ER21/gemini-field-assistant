## Services

### Storm Damage Roof Replacement

Our primary service. We inspect, document, and restore roofs damaged by hail, wind, ice, and storm events. Every project includes:

- Free detailed roof inspection with photo documentation
- Full damage report prepared for insurance submission
- Adjuster meeting attendance — we are there to represent the homeowner, not wait by the phone
- Negotiation of supplements for missed items, code upgrades, and O&L coverage
- Installation by factory-trained crews using GAF or CertainTeed systems
- Post-installation walkthrough and documentation packet

We do not send a salesperson and a separate crew. The person who assessed your roof understands your claim and communicates that context to the installation team.

### Roof Repair (Partial / Emergency)

For damage that does not require full replacement — or for emergency temporary repairs to prevent further interior damage while a claim is being processed. We apply the same documentation standard to repair work that we apply to full replacements.

### Siding Replacement

Storm damage to siding — hail impacts on aluminum, vinyl, wood, or fiber cement — is a covered loss under most homeowner policies. We handle siding claims with the same adjuster-engagement approach as roofing claims. Available in all three states. Siding claims frequently pair with roofing claims in the same storm event.

### Gutter Systems

Gutter and downspout replacement as part of a storm claim or as a standalone project. We install seamless aluminum gutters and can address fascia damage identified during gutter installation.

### Skylights and Penetrations

Skylight replacement and flashing repair as part of a broader roofing claim or as a standalone scope. Properly flashed penetrations are a common adjuster shortcut — we address missed penetration work in every supplement.

## The Claim Process — How We Work

Most homeowners have never navigated an insurance claim before. Our process is designed to carry them through it without confusion or gaps.

Step 1: Free Inspection
We inspect the roof at no charge. We document every area of damage with photos, measurements, and written notes. If there is claimable damage, we explain exactly what we found and why it warrants a claim.

Step 2: Claim Filing
We guide the homeowner through filing the claim with their insurer — what to say, what was damaged, and the date of loss. We provide all the information the homeowner needs before they make the call.

Step 3: Adjuster Meeting
We attend the adjuster inspection in person. This is the most important step. Adjusters work for the insurance company. We work for the homeowner. Our presence at the inspection ensures nothing significant is missed and the proper scope is documented from the beginning.

Step 4: Scope Review and Supplement
When the insurer's estimate arrives, we review it line by line against our own documentation. Missed items, code upgrades, O&L coverage, and manufacturer-required components are identified and submitted as a formal supplement.

Step 5: Approval and Contract
Once we achieve a full approval — covering the full scope of replacement at no out-of-pocket cost beyond the deductible — we proceed to contract and scheduling. If a full approval cannot be reached through negotiation, the homeowner owes us nothing.

Step 6: Installation
Our trained crews install the approved scope using GAF or CertainTeed systems meeting ASTM D3161, D7158, and UL standards. All work is performed to manufacturer specifications required for full warranty activation.

Step 7: Final Documentation
At project close, the homeowner receives a complete documentation packet: warranty certificates, permit documentation, before-and-after photos, and the installation completion certificate.

## Why Roof-ER

Competing quotes from storm-chasing contractors often look similar on paper. They are not the same in execution.

Certification matters. Roof-ER's CertainTeed ShingleMaster certification is the only path to CertainTeed's 4-Star and 5-Star warranties — offering up to 25 years of labor coverage and 130 mph wind rating. Non-certified contractors cannot offer this.

Adjuster expertise matters. Most contractors install roofs. We also negotiate claims. These are different skill sets. Our team has handled hundreds of adjuster meetings, supplement negotiations, and contested claim scenarios. We know the codes, the policy language, and the documentation standards adjusters cannot ignore.

Licensing matters. We are licensed in VA, MD, and PA. Many storm-chasing companies that follow major hail events into our territory operate without proper state licensing. An unlicensed contractor voids manufacturer warranties and creates liability for the homeowner.

Documentation matters. Insurance companies can and do deny claims due to inadequate documentation. We build the evidentiary record from day one — photographs, measurements, storm data, code references — so that our supplements and arguments are built on documented fact, not conversation.
