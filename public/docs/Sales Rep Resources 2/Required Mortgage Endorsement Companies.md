# Required Mortgage Endorsement Companies

## Overview

When a homeowner has a mortgage, the insurance company issues the claim check payable to both the homeowner AND the mortgage servicer (lienholder). Before the homeowner can use the funds, the mortgage company must endorse (sign off on) the check. This process is called the loss draft or mortgage endorsement process.

Failure to manage this process causes delays that stall jobs. Set expectations with the homeowner early. Engage the mortgage company the moment the Explanation of Benefits (EOB) or initial check arrives.

## How the Endorsement Process Works

### Standard Flow

1. Insurance company issues check payable to "Homeowner Name AND Mortgage Servicer."
2. Homeowner cannot cash or deposit the check without the mortgage company's endorsement.
3. Homeowner contacts the mortgage servicer's loss draft department (NOT general customer service — always loss draft specifically).
4. Servicer requires documentation before they will endorse:
   - Signed check (homeowner's endorsement only — do not have homeowner sign the mortgage company's line)
   - Contractor's license copy
   - Contractor's Certificate of Insurance (COI)
   - Contractor's W-9
   - Signed contract between homeowner and contractor
   - In some cases: contractor's written scope of work / estimate
5. Servicer reviews documents, may order their own inspection.
6. Servicer releases funds — either full amount, partial draw, or staged disbursement.

### Disbursement Models

Most mortgage servicers use one of these models based on claim size:

| Claim Amount | Typical Disbursement Model |
|---|---|
| Under $10,000 | One-time full release after document review |
| $10,000 — $40,000 | Two-draw: 50% at start, 50% at completion inspection |
| Over $40,000 | Three-draw: 33% at start, 33% at 50% completion, 34% at final inspection |

Note: Thresholds vary by servicer. Confirm the specific model with each servicer when submitting documentation.

## Typical Turnaround Times

| Servicer Tier | Expected Timeline |
|---|---|
| Portal-based (Mr. Cooper, Newrez, Rocket) | 5 — 10 business days after docs received |
| Phone/mail hybrid (Wells Fargo, Chase, BofA) | 10 — 21 business days |
| Smaller subservicers (Cenlar, LoanCare) | 15 — 30 business days |
| Complex / specialty loan servicers (SPS, SLS, Ocwen) | 21 — 45 business days |

Always add 5 business days buffer to any quoted timeline. Plan job scheduling around the realistic release date, not the quoted one.

## Tips for Navigating the Endorsement Process Faster

1. Start immediately. The moment the homeowner tells you a check is coming, call the mortgage servicer's loss draft line and find out exactly what documents they need. Do not wait for the check to arrive.

2. Prepare a contractor document packet in advance. Keep a ready PDF with your license, COI, and W-9. Attach it to every document submission immediately — do not make the servicer ask twice.

3. Use portals over phone/mail wherever available. Portal submissions are tracked, timestamped, and processed faster than mailed documents.

4. Follow up every 5 business days. Loss draft departments are understaffed. Calls reminder them the file is live and prevents it from sitting in a queue.

5. Confirm document receipt in writing. When submitting by mail, use certified mail. When submitting by portal, screenshot the confirmation.

6. Ask for a disbursement schedule on day one. Know in advance whether they will do full release or staged draws so you can plan your cash flow and job scheduling accordingly.

7. For large servicers with dedicated portals, register the claim before submitting documents. Creating the account and registering the check number early sometimes opens the file faster.

8. If the process stalls beyond their quoted timeline, escalate within the servicer. Ask for a supervisor in the loss draft department, not a general customer service rep.

9. Inform the homeowner at every stage. Homeowners get anxious when money is tied up with their mortgage company. A weekly update call from you manages their expectations and builds trust.

10. Do not begin work before confirming the first draw disbursement date. Starting work without funds confirmed creates collection risk for Roof-ER.

---

## Important: Never Instruct Homeowners to Sign the Mortgage Line

The check has two endorsement lines — one for the homeowner and one for the mortgage servicer. Instruct the homeowner NOT to sign the mortgage company's endorsement line. Only the mortgage company signs there. If the homeowner signs both lines, it creates a negotiation problem with the servicer and may require a stop-payment and reissue.
