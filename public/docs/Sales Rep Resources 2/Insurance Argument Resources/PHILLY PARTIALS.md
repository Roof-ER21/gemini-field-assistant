# PHILLY PARTIALS

## Overview

Philadelphia operates under a stricter and more complex permit and inspection regime than surrounding PA suburbs. Partial insurance approvals in Philadelphia require specific handling — the city's Department of Licenses and Inspections (L&I) frequently forces full-replacement outcomes that suburban townships do not. This works in the homeowner's favor when handled correctly.

---

## Key Difference: Philadelphia vs. PA Suburbs

In suburban PA townships, a partial roofing approval from insurance may be executable with a permit for that specific scope.

In Philadelphia, L&I applies the Philadelphia Building Code strictly, and partial re-roofing permits are routinely denied in favor of full deck-to-ridge replacement requirements. This means a partial insurance approval in Philly is almost always escalatable to a full replacement using the permit denial as evidence.

Do not treat a Philadelphia partial the same way you treat a suburban PA partial. The city's own code enforcement is your ally.

---

## Philadelphia L&I — What You Need to Know

Department: Department of Licenses and Inspections (L&I)
Website: phl.permits.com / phila.gov/li
Permit Portal: eCLIPSE (Philadelphia's online permit system)
Phone: (215) 686-2400

### Permit Requirements for Roofing in Philadelphia

- Any roofing work over 100 sq ft requires a permit in Philadelphia.
- Partial re-roofing (replacing only a portion of the roof) requires a permit for the specific scope.
- L&I inspectors apply Philadelphia's amendments to the International Residential Code (IRC 2018 as locally amended).
- Philadelphia Code Section PM-506 and Building Code Sections 1507 require that re-roofing work meet current code — including deck integrity, underlayment, and ice/water shield requirements.
- If the existing deck is damaged, deteriorated, or non-code-compliant, L&I will typically require a full deck replacement before issuing a certificate of occupancy for roofing work.

### The Partial Denial Sequence (How Philly Works for You)

1. Insurance approves only a partial (e.g., one slope or one section).
2. You apply for a permit at L&I for the partial scope.
3. L&I inspector reviews and cites code compliance issues with partial replacement (deck, flashing, code upgrades).
4. L&I issues a denial or requires full replacement as a condition of permit.
5. You obtain the written denial/requirement from L&I.
6. You submit the L&I documentation to the insurance adjuster with a formal supplement request.
7. Adjuster is now legally obligated to address the code-required full replacement under the policy's Code Upgrade / Ordinance or Law coverage.

This sequence is well-established in Philadelphia. Do not skip straight to step 6 — you need the written L&I documentation.

---

## Ordinance or Law Coverage — The Philadelphia Lever

Most homeowner insurance policies include Ordinance or Law (O&L) coverage, which pays for code upgrades mandated by local government when a covered loss triggers repairs. In Philadelphia:

- O&L typically covers 10–25% of dwelling coverage (check the specific policy).
- It can be used to fund the gap between a partial approval and a full code-compliant replacement.
- If L&I requires a full deck replacement, that is a code-mandated upgrade — the insurer must pay under O&L.
- If the insurer disputes the O&L applicability, cite the specific L&I notice and the policy's O&L endorsement language.

Key script for the adjuster:
"The City of Philadelphia's Department of Licenses and Inspections has issued a written requirement that the partial replacement cannot be permitted without bringing the full assembly to current code. The policy's Ordinance or Law provision applies. We are requesting a supplement to cover the full code-compliant replacement."

## Step-by-Step: Handling a Philly Partial

1. Receive partial approval from insurer.
2. Notify Team Leader immediately — Philadelphia partials always get Team Leader involvement.
3. Apply for the permit in eCLIPSE for the approved partial scope only.
4. Attend the L&I inspection. Bring photos of deck condition, existing flashing, and all damage documentation.
5. Obtain written denial or written condition requiring full replacement from L&I.
6. Draft supplement letter citing:
   - The L&I written notice (attach as exhibit)
   - Specific Philadelphia Building Code sections
   - Policy O&L endorsement language
   - Full replacement cost estimate
7. Submit supplement to adjuster. Give 15-business-day response window.
8. If adjuster denies or delays, escalate to Pennsylvania Insurance Department complaint process.

| Step | Estimated Timeframe |
|---|---|
| eCLIPSE permit application submitted | Day 1 |
| Initial review (residential roofing) | 3 — 10 business days |
| L&I site inspection scheduled | 5 — 15 business days after approval |
| Written determination issued | 3 — 7 business days post-inspection |

Note: Philadelphia permit timelines vary significantly. Expedited review is available for an additional fee. Coordinate with the homeowner on timeline expectations.

## Common L&I Code Citations Used in Philly Partials

- Philadelphia IRC Section R905.2 — Asphalt shingles: requires compliant deck, underlayment, and fastening.
- Philadelphia BC Section 1509 — Roof replacement must meet current code when more than 25% of the roof is replaced.
- Philadelphia PM Code Section PM-506 — Roofing systems must be structurally sound and watertight; damaged deck triggers full compliance review.
- Ice and water shield requirement: Philadelphia requires ice and water shield at eaves for the full roof assembly when re-roofing, not just the repaired section.

## What NOT to Do in Philadelphia

- Do not begin any partial work before the permit situation is resolved.
- Do not tell the homeowner the partial is acceptable — it almost never is under Philly code.
- Do not submit the supplement without the L&I written notice attached.
- Do not apply for a full-replacement permit before attempting the partial permit first — you need the denial documentation.
