# Maryland Residential Building Codes — Re-Roofing & Building Envelope

Maryland adopts the International Residential Code (IRC) 2021 through the Maryland Building Performance Standards (COMAR 09.12.01). All residential roofing work must comply with IRC 2021 as amended by Maryland.

Reference: Maryland Residential Code 2021 (Adopts with Amendments: International Residential Code 2021)

---

## R908.2 Structural and Construction Loads

The structural roof components shall be capable of supporting the roof covering system and the material and equipment loads that will be encountered during installation of the roof covering system.

**Insurance argument**: If an adjuster approves a recover/overlay but the structural analysis shows the framing cannot support the additional weight, a full tear-off is required by code. The carrier must cover the tear-off cost as a code-mandated requirement.

## R908.3 Roof Replacement (CRITICAL — Maryland Requires Full Tear-Off)

**"Roof replacement shall include the removal of existing layers of roof coverings down to the roof deck."**

This is the single most important code section for Maryland roofing claims. Unlike some states that permit overlaying one layer over another, **Maryland IRC R908.3 requires complete tear-off to the roof deck for any roof replacement**.

**Exception**: Where the existing roof assembly includes an ice barrier membrane that is adhered to the roof deck, the existing ice barrier membrane shall be permitted to remain in place and covered with an additional layer of ice barrier membrane in accordance with Section R905.

### What This Means for Insurance Claims

1. **No overlays in Maryland** — If the insurance company tries to authorize a recover/overlay instead of a tear-off, cite R908.3 directly. Maryland code does not permit installing new shingles over existing shingles when performing a roof replacement.
2. **Tear-off is code-required, not an upgrade** — The cost of removing existing roofing materials is a code compliance cost, not an optional upgrade. The insurance company cannot deny tear-off costs when the work constitutes a roof replacement.
3. **Ice barrier exception is narrow** — The only existing material permitted to remain is an ice barrier membrane adhered to the deck. All shingles, underlayment, and other coverings must be removed.

## IRC R908 — Exact Code Language (IRC 2021 as adopted by Maryland MBPS)

Maryland adopts IRC 2021 R908 **without amendments** — the text below is the law.

### R908.3 Roof Replacement
**"Roof replacement shall include the removal of existing layers of roof coverings down to the roof deck."**

**Exception:** Existing ice barrier membrane adhered to deck may remain if covered with new layer per R905.

### R908.3.1 Roof Recover — Permitted ONLY When:
1. Installed per manufacturer's approved instructions
2. Standing-seam metal systems transmitting loads directly to structure
3. Metal panel/shingle or tile over existing wood shake per R908.4
4. New protective coating over existing coating/metal/BUR/modified bitumen/single-ply/SPF

### R908.3.1.1 Roof Recover NOT Allowed — SHALL NOT be permitted when:
1. Existing roof is **water soaked or deteriorated** to the point it is not adequate as a base
2. Existing covering is **slate, clay, cement, or asbestos-cement tile**
3. Existing roof has **two or more applications** of any type of roof covering

### R908.5 Reinstallation of Materials
Existing flashings, edgings, outlets, vents shall be **replaced** where rusted, damaged, or deteriorated.

### R908.6 Flashings
Flashings shall be reconstructed per manufacturer's installation instructions. Metal flashing receiving bituminous materials shall be primed before installation.

## Why R908.3 Is Your Best Weapon in Maryland

R908.3 is absolute: **roof replacement means tear-off to the deck. Period.**

If an adjuster or insurance company tries to authorize a "recover" or "overlay" instead of a full tear-off, the response is simple:

**"Maryland Residential Code R908.3 states that roof replacement shall include the removal of existing layers of roof coverings down to the roof deck. This is not optional — it is the law."**

The ONLY exception is an existing ice barrier membrane adhered to the deck may remain in place (R908.3 Exception). Everything else — shingles, underlayment, felt — must come off.

**When an adjuster argues for repair instead of replacement:**
- If storm damage affects multiple slopes or a significant portion of the roof, the scope of work becomes a replacement, which triggers R908.3 full tear-off
- If the existing roof has any layers beyond the original, R908.3.1.1 prohibits a recover — tear-off is mandatory
- If deck damage is suspected, tear-off is required to expose and inspect/repair the deck
- If existing roof is water soaked or deteriorated, R908.3.1.1 prohibits a recover
- All new roofing must meet current code — ice barrier, drip edge, flashings — even if the original installation didn't have them

## Ice & Water Shield (R905.1.2 / R905.2.7.1)

- Required at eaves: self-adhering polymer-modified bitumen sheet from eave edge to **24 inches past the interior face of the exterior wall**.
- Required in **all valleys**.
- Required at **roof-to-wall intersections**.
- Must comply with ASTM D1970.
- **Maryland enforces ice barrier in all jurisdictions**.

## House Wrap / Weather-Resistive Barrier (R703.1, R703.2)

- **Required** behind all exterior wall cladding (siding, brick, stucco, fiber cement).
- Minimum: One layer of **#15 asphalt felt** (ASTM D226 Type I) or approved housewrap.
- Applied horizontally, upper over lower lapped **not less than 2 inches**.
- Joints lapped **not less than 6 inches**.
- Must be continuous to top of walls.
- When re-siding after storm damage, if existing WRB is damaged or absent, **new WRB is code-required** — not an upgrade.

### R703.7.3 Enhanced WRB for Stucco/EIFS

When applied over wood-based sheathing, the WRB must include a **water-resistive vapor-permeable barrier** with performance at least equivalent to **two layers of Grade D paper**. Each layer must provide a separate continuous plane.

## Flashings (R903.2.1, R908.5)

- Required at all wall-to-roof intersections, slope changes, penetrations, and roof perimeter.
- **Step flashing**: minimum 4" wide, 4" up wall, 4" on roof at every wall-to-roof junction.
- **Counter flashing**: over step flashing at masonry walls.
- **Apron flashing**: at base of walls where roof meets wall below.
- **During re-roofing (R908.5)**: All existing flashings that are corroded, damaged, or improperly installed **must be replaced**. On a full tear-off, **all flashings must be replaced**.
- Self-adhered membranes: must comply with AAMA 711.

## Cricket / Saddle (R903.2.2)

- Required on ridge side of chimney or penetration **more than 30 inches wide**.
- Must be covered with metal flashing or same material as roof.

## Drip Edge (R905.2.8.5)

- Required at **eaves and rakes** on all asphalt shingle roofs.
- Minimum 0.25" below sheathing, 2" back on deck.
- Corrosion-resistant metal (min 26-gauge galvanized).
- At eave: **under** underlayment. At rake: **over** underlayment.

## Ventilation (R806.1)

- Enclosed attics: minimum **1/150** net free ventilating area.
- Reduced to **1/300** with Class I/II vapor retarder OR balanced ridge/soffit ventilation.
- Do not mix exhaust vent types.

## Underlayment (R905.1.1)

- **Slopes 4:12+**: minimum one layer #15 felt or approved synthetic.
- **Slopes 2:12 to 4:12**: double coverage #15 felt OR single self-adhering modified bitumen over entire surface.
- **Slopes less than 2:12**: asphalt shingles NOT permitted.

## Permits (Maryland specific)

- A **building permit is required** for roof replacement in all Maryland jurisdictions.
- Montgomery County, Prince George's County, Baltimore County, and City of Baltimore each have their own permit offices.
- Permit fees vary by jurisdiction (typically $75-$200 for residential roof replacement).
- **Permit is the contractor's responsibility**, not the homeowner's.
- Work without a permit can void insurance coverage and create liability issues.

## Maryland Contractor Licensing

- Maryland Home Improvement Commission (MHIC) license **required** for all residential roofing work.
- MHIC license number must appear on all contracts and advertising.
- Operating without MHIC license: fines up to $5,000 per violation.
- Verify at: https://www.dllr.state.md.us/license/mhic/

## MD vs. VA vs. PA — R908 Is Identical

R908 (Reroofing) is adopted **without amendment** by all three states. The code text is identical. The differences are in how each state enforces it and what additional requirements apply.

| Topic | Maryland | Virginia | Pennsylvania |
|-------|----------|---------|--------------|
| R908 text | IRC 2021 — no amendments | IRC 2021 — no amendments | IRC 2018 — no amendments |
| Enforcement | Strong — MBPS | Moderate — USBC | Strong — UCC + local L&I |
| Matching argument | **YES — R908.3 + uniform appearance** | Context-dependent (check policy) | **NO — use permit denial strategy** |
| Contractor license | MHIC required | DPOR Class A/B/C required | HIC required |
| Best leverage | R908.3 tear-off requirement | Manufacturer specs + repairability | L&I permit denial |
