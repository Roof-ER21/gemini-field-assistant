# Virginia Residential Building Codes

Virginia adopts the International Residential Code (IRC) through the Virginia Uniform Statewide Building Code (USBC), 2021 edition (effective July 1, 2021). All residential roofing work in Virginia must comply with these provisions.

---

## Ice & Water Shield (R905.1.2 / R905.2.7.1)

- **Required at eaves**: Self-adhering polymer-modified bitumen sheet must extend from the eave edge to at least **24 inches past the interior face of the exterior wall**.
- **Required in valleys**: Ice barrier underlayment must extend **36 inches from the centerline of the valley**.
- **Required at roof-to-wall intersections**: Where a roof abuts a vertical wall.
- Virginia enforces this in **all jurisdictions** — no local exemptions.
- Must comply with ASTM D1970 (self-adhering polymer modified bitumen sheet).

## Underlayment Requirements (R905.1.1)

- **Slopes 4:12 and greater**: Minimum one layer of #15 asphalt felt (ASTM D226 Type I) or approved synthetic underlayment.
- **Slopes 2:12 to less than 4:12 (low slope)**: Must use double coverage of #15 felt or a single layer of self-adhering modified bitumen underlayment over the entire roof surface.
- **Slopes less than 2:12**: Asphalt shingles are **NOT permitted** — must use built-up, modified bitumen, or single-ply membrane roofing.

## House Wrap / Weather-Resistive Barrier (R703.1, R703.2)

- A **weather-resistive barrier (WRB)** is **required** behind all exterior wall cladding — this includes vinyl siding, fiber cement, stucco, brick veneer, and wood siding.
- **Minimum standard**: One layer of **#15 asphalt felt** (ASTM D226 Type I) or an **approved housewrap** (e.g., Tyvek, Typar) free from holes and breaks.
- Must be applied **shingle-fashion** (horizontally), upper layer lapped over lower layer **not less than 2 inches**.
- Where joints occur, felt shall be lapped **not less than 6 inches**.
- Must be **continuous to the top of walls** and properly terminated at penetrations, windows, doors, and building appendages.
- **Virginia does NOT exempt any cladding type** from the WRB requirement — even if the manufacturer claims self-waterproofing, a separate WRB is still code-required.

### House Wrap and Re-Siding

- When replacing siding (re-siding), if the existing WRB is damaged, deteriorated, or absent, a **new WRB must be installed** before new siding is applied.
- Insurance adjusters sometimes deny house wrap on siding claims — Virginia code **R703.2 requires it**. This is not optional.
- If storm damage removed or compromised the existing WRB, the WRB replacement is a **code-required** line item, not an upgrade.

## Cricket / Saddle (R903.2.2)

- A **cricket or saddle** is **required** on the ridge side of any chimney or penetration that is **more than 30 inches wide** (measured perpendicular to the slope).
- The cricket must be covered with **metal flashing** or the same roofing material as the roof.
- Cricket/saddle flashing must direct water away from the chimney and toward the roof drainage path.

## Flashings — Step, Apron, Chimney (R903.2.1)

- **Flashing is required** at all wall-to-roof intersections, changes in roof slope, around all roof penetrations (vents, pipes, skylights), and at the perimeter of the roof where it meets vertical surfaces.
- **Step flashing**: Required at every wall-to-roof junction. Each piece must be at least **4 inches wide** and extend at least **4 inches up the wall** and **4 inches onto the roof deck**.
- **Counter flashing**: Must be installed over step flashing at masonry walls (e.g., brick chimneys). Must be embedded or regletted into the masonry joint.
- **Apron flashing**: Required at the base of vertical walls where a roof slope meets the wall below.
- **Chimney flashing**: Step flashing on sides, apron/base flashing at the front, counter flashing over step flashing, and a **cricket/saddle** if the chimney exceeds 30 inches wide.
- All flashing must be **corrosion-resistant** (galvanized steel, aluminum, copper, or lead).
- Self-adhered membranes used as flashing must comply with **AAMA 711**.

## Drip Edge (R905.2.8.5)

- **Drip edge is required** at eaves and rakes (gable ends) on all asphalt shingle roofs.
- Must extend **not less than 0.25 inches below the roof sheathing** and back on the roof deck **not less than 2 inches**.
- Must be corrosion-resistant metal (minimum 26-gauge galvanized steel or equivalent).
- At the eave: installed **under** the underlayment. At the rake: installed **over** the underlayment.

## Ventilation Requirements (R806.1)

- **Enclosed attics and enclosed rafter spaces** must have cross ventilation for each separate space.
- Minimum **net free ventilating area**: 1/150 of the area of the vented space.
- Reduced to **1/300** if a Class I or II vapor retarder is installed on the warm side OR if at least 40% but not more than 50% of ventilation is provided by ventilators located in the upper portion of the space (ridge or near-ridge).
- Balanced ventilation: intake (soffit) and exhaust (ridge/roof) vents should be approximately equal.
- **Do NOT mix exhaust vent types** (e.g., ridge vent + turbine vent = short-circuit risk).

## Roof Slope Requirements (R905.2.2)

- Asphalt shingles: **Minimum 2:12 slope** with additional underlayment per R905.1.1.
- Standard application (no special underlayment): **Minimum 4:12 slope**.
- Manufacturer installation instructions must be followed per **R905.2.1**.

## Nailing / Fastening (R905.2.5)

- Asphalt shingles must be fastened with **minimum 4 nails per shingle** (6 nails in high-wind areas or on slopes exceeding 60 degrees / mansard).
- Nails must penetrate **at least 3/4 inch into the roof sheathing** or through the thickness of the sheathing, whichever is less.
- In Virginia, areas designated as **high wind** (110+ mph design wind speed) require 6-nail pattern.

---

## Key Virginia-Specific Notes

1. Virginia uses the **2021 USBC** (based on 2021 IRC for residential) as of July 1, 2021.
2. Local jurisdictions may NOT weaken code requirements but **may adopt more restrictive provisions**.
3. All roofing work requiring a permit must be performed by a **licensed contractor** (Class A, B, or C based on project value).
4. **Permits are required** for roof replacement (not for repairs of less than 100 sq ft or emergency repairs).
5. When re-roofing, the new installation must meet **current code** — not the code in effect when the original roof was installed.
