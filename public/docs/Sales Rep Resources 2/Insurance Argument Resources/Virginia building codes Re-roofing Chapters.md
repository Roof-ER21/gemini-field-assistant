# Virginia Building Codes — Re-Roofing Chapters

2021 Virginia Uniform Statewide Building Code (USBC), based on 2021 IRC Chapter 9 and Virginia Existing Building Code (VEBC) Chapter 6.

Reference: https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/VAEBC2021P1/chapter-6-alterations

---

## Re-Roofing Overview (IRC R908)

Re-roofing refers to the installation of new roof covering over or in place of existing roof covering. Virginia follows IRC Section R908 with state amendments.

## Double-Layered Roofs / Roof Recover (R908.3)

### When Overlay (Recover) Is Permitted

- **Maximum 2 layers total**: Virginia permits installing **one additional layer** of asphalt shingles over **one existing layer** of asphalt shingles (2 layers total maximum).
- The existing roof must be **asphalt shingles only** — overlaying over wood shakes, wood shingles, slate, tile, metal, or any non-asphalt material is **NOT permitted**.
- The existing roof surface must be **reasonably smooth and flat** — no severe curling, cupping, or buckling.
- **All deteriorated or damaged sections** of the existing roof must be removed and the deck repaired before overlay.

### When Complete Tear-Off Is REQUIRED (R908.3.1)

A complete tear-off (removal of all existing roof coverings down to the deck) is **required** when:

1. **Two or more layers** of asphalt shingles already exist on the roof.
2. The existing roof covering is **wood shake or wood shingle** — cannot overlay with asphalt shingles over wood products.
3. The existing roof covering is **slate, clay tile, concrete tile, or metal** — these must be fully removed.
4. The **roof deck is damaged, deteriorated, or structurally inadequate** — deck must be exposed and repaired/replaced.
5. The existing shingles show evidence of **moisture damage, mold, or rot** in the sheathing below.
6. The building is being **re-roofed with a different material type** than what is currently installed.
7. When required by the **roofing manufacturer's installation instructions** for warranty compliance.

### Structural Verification

- Before overlaying, the **roof framing must be verified** to support the combined weight of existing and new roofing materials.
- Typical asphalt shingle weight: **2-3 lbs per square foot per layer**.
- Two layers = approximately **5-6 lbs/sq ft** of dead load from roofing alone.
- If there is any question of structural adequacy, a structural evaluation is required.
- **Insurance argument**: If an adjuster authorizes overlay instead of tear-off, the contractor can cite structural concerns and code compliance as grounds for full replacement.

## Flashing Requirements During Re-Roofing (R908.5)

- **New flashings are required** wherever the existing flashings are corroded, damaged, or improperly installed.
- When performing a tear-off, **all flashings must be replaced** — step, counter, apron, valley, chimney, pipe boots, and drip edge.
- When overlaying, existing flashings must be **inspected and replaced if deteriorated**.
- Valley flashing for re-roofing: must extend **at least 24 inches wide** (12 inches each side of valley centerline).
- Virginia code requires flashing at all roof-to-wall intersections regardless of whether it was present on the original installation.

## Underlayment During Re-Roofing (R908.6)

- When a complete tear-off is performed, **new underlayment must be installed** per current code (R905.1.1).
- Ice & water shield must be installed at eaves, valleys, and roof-to-wall intersections per current R905.1.2 — even if the original roof did not have ice barrier (must be brought up to **current code**).
- Synthetic underlayment is acceptable if it meets manufacturer specifications and is approved by the local AHJ.

## Permits and Inspections (R108)

- **A permit IS required** for roof replacement and re-roofing in Virginia.
- **A permit is NOT required** for:
  - Repairs to an existing roof covering (less than 100 sq ft)
  - Emergency repairs to protect from weather damage (must apply for permit within a reasonable time)
- **Final inspection** is required before the roof is considered code-compliant.
- The **contractor** (not the homeowner) is responsible for pulling the permit when the contractor performs the work.

## Insurance Claim Implications

### Arguing for Full Replacement vs. Partial Repair

1. **If 2 layers exist**: Full tear-off is **code-required** — no overlay option. Insurance must cover the tear-off cost as it is a code-mandated requirement, not an upgrade.
2. **If shingles are discontinued**: Matching requirement — if the existing shingle is no longer manufactured, a full slope or full roof replacement may be required to achieve uniform appearance per Virginia matching principles.
3. **If deck damage is suspected**: Tear-off is required to expose and repair the deck. Insurance should cover exploratory tear-off if storm damage is documented.
4. **Code upgrade costs**: When re-roofing triggers code compliance (ice & water shield, drip edge, ventilation that wasn't originally installed), these are **code-required items**, not upgrades. Present to adjuster as "code compliance" line items.

### Virginia Roofing Law — Contractor Licensing

- **Class A**: Projects over $120,000 — requires RBC (Residential Building Contractor) or CIC (Commercial/Industrial Contractor) designation.
- **Class B**: Projects $10,000 to $120,000.
- **Class C**: Projects $1,000 to $10,000.
- All contractors must have a **Virginia contractor's license** issued by DPOR (Department of Professional and Occupational Regulation).
- Operating without a license: Class 1 misdemeanor, fines up to $2,500 per offense.
- Homeowners should verify contractor license at: https://www.dpor.virginia.gov

### IRC R908 — Exact Code Language (IRC 2021 as adopted by Virginia USBC)

**R908.3 Roof Replacement:**
"Roof replacement shall include the removal of existing layers of roof coverings down to the roof deck."
Exception: Existing ice barrier membrane adhered to deck may remain if covered with new layer per R905.

**R908.3.1 Roof Recover — Permitted When:**
1. Installed per manufacturer's approved instructions
2. Standing-seam metal systems transmitting loads directly to structure
3. Metal panel/shingle or tile over existing wood shake per R908.4
4. New protective coating over existing coating/metal/BUR/modified bitumen/single-ply/SPF

**R908.3.1.1 Roof Recover NOT Allowed — SHALL NOT be permitted when:**
1. Existing roof is **water soaked or deteriorated** to the point it is not adequate as a base
2. Existing covering is **slate, clay, cement, or asbestos-cement tile**
3. Existing roof has **two or more applications** of any type of roof covering

**R908.5 Reinstallation of Materials:**
Existing flashings, edgings, outlets, vents shall be **replaced** where rusted, damaged, or deteriorated. Aggregate surfacing shall not be reinstalled.

**R908.6 Flashings:**
Flashings shall be reconstructed per manufacturer's installation instructions. Metal flashing receiving bituminous materials shall be primed before installation.

## Adjuster Talking Points

When an insurance adjuster tries to authorize a repair instead of replacement, or an overlay instead of tear-off, the following code-based arguments apply:

1. **"Code requires tear-off"** — If 2+ layers exist or the existing material is not asphalt shingles (R908.3.1).
2. **"Code requires full underlayment"** — On any tear-off, ice barrier and underlayment must be installed per current code, even if the original roof lacked them (R908.6, R905.1.1, R905.1.2).
3. **"Code requires new flashings"** — On tear-off, all flashings must be replaced. On overlay, deteriorated flashings must be replaced (R908.5).
4. **"Code requires drip edge"** — Must be installed at eaves and rakes on all asphalt shingle installations (R905.2.8.5).
5. **"Code requires a cricket"** — If chimney exceeds 30 inches in width (R903.2.2).
6. **"Structural concern with overlay"** — Combined weight of 2 layers may exceed framing capacity; structural evaluation needed before overlay can be approved.
7. **"Manufacturer won't warranty an overlay"** — Most shingle manufacturers void or reduce warranty coverage when installed over existing shingles.
