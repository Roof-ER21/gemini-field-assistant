# Essentials For a Successful Pitch

## Before Every Pitch
1. Know what product you are pitching
2. Knock loud and long enough for them to hear it, but do not scare or annoy them
3. Carry relaxed body language, hold eye contact, and smile & wave

## How You Should Feel at the Door
- **Indifferent** to the outcome — not every homeowner wants a free quote, move to the next house
- **Confident** in what you are saying — make them really believe the roof is being done for their neighbor
- **Helpful** — you are not selling anything, you are pointing out potential issues while also offering a free solution
- You should NOT have to convince homeowners that they need the quote

## How the Homeowner Feels
They feel: skeptical, angry, trapped, bothered.

They SHOULD feel:
- **At ease** — they understand you are not a threat or selling them anything (talk slowly, get to the point quickly)
- **Neutral-Happy** — even if they don't sign up, are they still saying "I appreciate it" or "thanks for coming by"?
- **Helped** — is the interaction positive or negative?

## Your Message
- You are just giving the neighbors a heads up
- Our company is doing the "product" for your neighbor
- We are just coming by and doing free quotes for everyone

## Common Problems to Avoid
- **Bad pitch/tone/vibe** — don't let them know you are pitching them, talk like a person
- **Talking too much** — get to the point, A → B
- **Not sounding confident/Not smiling** — if you are not smiling, the people at the door will feel awkward
- **Poor framing** — you are just pointing out potential issues and providing a free solution

## Must Do's
1. Know what you are pitching before you pitch
2. Talk slow and control the conversation
3. Be able to answer any questions on the spot
4. Never sound unsure/confused — all credibility will be lost
5. Read every conversation/day differently (what town? how did they open the door?)
6. **Be Present At The Door** — listen to what they are saying
7. If they don't say "No, Not Interested, or I don't want a quote", always ask twice but frame it differently

## Common Responses
- "I just got a new roof" → Pivot to other products
- "Can I just get a flyer?" → "Absolutely, my job is simple..."
- "No, I can't do it right now" → Use the "Busy" rebuttal

## Long Term Vision
- Understand what's working for you
- Build a foundation of knowledge on ALL products
- Get 1% better each day
- Be aware of the vibe you are giving off
