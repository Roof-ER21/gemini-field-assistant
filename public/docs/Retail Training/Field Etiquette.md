# Field Etiquette — Little Details That Make a Big Difference

| # | Tip | What It Means & Why It Works |
|---|-----|------------------------------|
| 1 | **Smile** | Disarms tension. Friendly wins. |
| 2 | **Wave** | Builds trust from a distance. Humanizes you. |
| 3 | **Doorbell Sandwich** | Knock – pause – ring doorbell – knock again. Avoids startling. |
| 4 | **Broomstick Theory** | Take two big steps back. Give space = earn respect. |
| 5 | **Calvin Klein** | Stand and present calmly, confidently, disarming. Dress like a pro. Look clean, sharp, trustworthy. |
| 6 | **Project Your Voice** | Be heard confidently — don't mumble or whisper. |
| 7 | **Talk Slow** | Helps you sound calm, credible, non-salesy, and intentional. |
| 8 | **Pregnant Pause** | Pause after key statements and questions. Let silence build curiosity and engagement. |
| 9 | **Indifference** | "I'm not here to sell — just to share." Take the pressure off the homeowner. "I'm just letting you know." Statements, not asking. |
| 10 | **Don't Look Inside** | Keep your eyes outside. It's respectful and builds trust fast. Plus, they can't simply wave you off from inside before answering the door. |

## Why This Matters
How homeowners perceive you in the first 5 seconds determines everything. These aren't tricks — they're habits that make you approachable, professional, and trustworthy. Practice them until they're automatic.
